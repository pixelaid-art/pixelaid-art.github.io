restoration of old photos.

Each of us in the photo album will find in his own way a unique memorable photo that has lost its original appearance under the influence of time and any external factors.

Even when stored very carefully, images fade and the paper becomes cracked. Thanks to modern technology, it has become possible to give old photographs, which are a family heirloom and keep valuable memories, a second life.

Each of the photographs is individual and undoubtedly requires an individual approach of the master. We will carry out the restoration of your photos of any complexity!

Restoration Level 1

  • A faded photo requiring color and tone correction, contrast restoration.
  • Removal of small scratches and stains, restoration of non-critical damage to image details.
  • Reconstruction of a homogeneous background.

Restoration Level 2

  • Severely faded photo, many small scratches, and cracks.
  • Elimination of large-scale damage that does not significantly affect important image details (body parts, especially the face).
  • Restoration of a uniform or uncomplicated background and minor damage to clothing.

Restoration Level 3

  • Large-scale damage to the photograph, such as missing fragments of important elements (on the face and body parts, complex background elements).
  • This kind of restoration requires great skill from the artist-restorer since in this case the lost fragments of the image are drawn by hand on a graphic tablet.

In cases of serious damage in the image of areas affecting the face, it is advisable to have additional photographs of the person depicted in the photograph so that the master can more accurately recreate his appearance.

colorize black and white photos.

In addition to the restoration of photographs, we also perform colorization (coloring) of black and white photographs. The technique of colorizing monochrome images allows you to give photographs from a bygone era a fresh, modern look.

Black and white photographs do not carry any information about color, therefore, coloring monochrome photographs is a very creative process, performed by the wizard with both manual and automatic digital image processing tools. A color overlay cannot accurately reproduce the real colors and, in many ways, the result depends on the imagination and talent of the artist.

Colorization is an imitation of a color photograph, and of course, a colorized photograph will not be completely identical to a photograph originally taken in color. But with high-quality coloring, not everyone can find the differences. And, undoubtedly, the performed colorization will allow you to look at your old pictures in a new way.
